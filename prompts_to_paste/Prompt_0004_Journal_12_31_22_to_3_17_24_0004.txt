Reimagining heaven and hell

Heaven and hell begin on earth, begin at our birth
Eternal heaven and hell are a continuation of what they are on earth

I hate the  idea of hell but try as I might I have not been able to interpret it out of the Bible.  Jesus makes it very clear that hell is a reality.  Therefore I choose to align myself with scripture instead of placing myself over it, and accepting the reality of hell.  But I want to know as much as I can about heaven and hell to best inform my decisions and to accurately share the gospel with the world.

We must always consider heaven and hell together, because they represent the perennial choice god gives us to walk in light or walk in darkness, to grow in love or to descend into hatred.  This is a daily and even minute by minute sober decision for those walking into heaven but it is also a great responsibility to let others know about the gravity of these decisions and their ultimate consequences.
Hell is an evidence of God’s love.  God does not force us to love him but allows us free will.  If we reject his authority in our lives, would prefer to be the captain of our own lives, would choose to live apart from God; then we will receive our desire.  Unfortunately what so many who choose to be separated from God don’t realize is that everything good in their life, in the world and in the universe is an emanation from the creativity, source energy and love of God.  Apart from him,  we will no longer experience the warmth of sunlight on our backs, the laughter of children,  the delight of good food,  the ecstasy of sexuality and the joy of friendship.  Hell is a vacuum of all those things.  The gifts cannot be separated from the giver.
To the extent that you are experiencing goodness in this life, you are experiencing the goodness of eternal life in the presence of God.  To the extent that you are experiencing frustration, despair, loneliness and self hatred, you are experiencing a foretaste of hell.  The Bible calls this dichotomy life versus death.  God is the author of life and satan is the author of death.
God’s righteous anger and his holy judgments and his blessing and loving kindness are the natural consequences of our behavior.   If we give little we receive little.  If we give much, we receive much.  If we try to take advantage of other people, we will sow mistrust.  If we serve people, we will reap trust and affection.
