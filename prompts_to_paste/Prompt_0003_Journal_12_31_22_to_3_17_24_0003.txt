The life of an infant
A baby instinctively knows to cry out for any reason it is uncomfortable or in pain: hungry, wet, itchy, hot, cold, separated, afraid.  This crucial instinct can be deliberately or undeliberately trained out of the infant.  When an infant’s needs are promptly attended to, an infant feels safe, loved and develops a a solid sense of being.  This healthy sense of self is more difficult to instill in a child the older they become.  A weak sense of self instilled in an infant can be recovered by a toddler who is unconditionally loved.
Developmental task is to develop secure attachment to primary caregivers

# Agency
We don’t learn to love ourselves until we develop a true sense of agency.  Without it, we may feel love when circumstances are favorable, but lose that sense of love when circumstances are less than favorable.  When we have agency, we choose our thoughts, feelings and actions and we can choose to experience love whenever we desire it.
I think part of my euphoria in mania is that I  feel a high sense of agency which is unshakeable by my circumstances.  Agency gives a person a deep sense of power, first to choose one’s own internal landscape and to also love and influence those around you.  This power is the presence of the divine within you.  For God is the procreator, initiator, first and last mover.  What he desires is.

# The crux of faith
God is the person of ultimate consequence.  He is the greatest authority in the universe.
He is immutable, omniscient, omnipotent, omnipresent
Will we submit to him or will we choose to elevate ourselves to the position of God.
God is the source of everything good in the world.  If we choose to turn from God than we are denying ourselves access to the infinite resources of God.

A major cause of faithlessness is the problem of evil which is no problem at all.  Humanity introduced evil in the world when we decided to be arbiters of what is good and evil instead of aligning ourselves to reality which is established by God.  We have the false belief that other people are more evil than we are and we determine that we should have special treatment, that we should be immune to suffering and loss. There are no good people only people that may do good and will always do evil.  When sin is conquered the good will be those who submit to God.
As a sign of our submission to God, we are called to submit to every earthly authority.  If we cannot submit to earthly authority that we can see, we will be unable to submit to God whom we cannot see.

Jesus Christ modeled submission by submitting to his father and to earthly authorities.  His obedience to the cross was the ultimate sacrifice that God was waiting for.  Jesus to within himself the condemnation and sting of sin and death that spared us from God.  We can now approach the throne of Grace and be righteous in his eyes.  Believing in Jesus is the first act of faith that makes us righteous in the bite of God.

We learn obedience through suffering

Suffering is not proof that God doesn’t exist or doesn’t love us,  it is proof of the condition of sin and brokenness in ourselves and in the world.  It is actually proof that God loves us because he uses it to make us stronger and to show his comfort and love to us, he uses it to give us compassion for others, he uses it to test our obedience, he uses it to demonstrate to us the utter corruption of sin, he uses it to purify us

Your greatest ministry will come out of your deepest pain,
Adversity grows community
Our weakness is our witness
The greatest witness of Jesus’s love was not hits teaching or his miracles and ministry.  It was his chosen suffering
Rick Warren
